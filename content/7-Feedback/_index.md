## OVERALL EVALUATION OF THE INTERNSHIP AT FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY (FCAJ) BOOTCAMP

### 1. Working Environment

During my time in the FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY (FCAJ) Bootcamp, I found it to be a very professional yet friendly learning environment. Although the program is organized as a bootcamp rather than a traditional company, the learning process and project execution were designed to closely resemble a real working environment. Mentors, Teaching Assistants (TAs), and fellow trainees were always ready to support one another whenever difficulties arose, creating an open and positive learning atmosphere.

What impressed me most was that everyone was encouraged to take initiative in learning and sharing knowledge. Whenever I faced challenges while implementing a project, I always received support from mentors as well as team members. This not only helped me solve problems but also motivated me to keep learning and developing myself.

---

### 2. Support from Mentors and the Team Admin

Throughout the internship, I received very dedicated guidance from the mentors. Whenever I encountered difficulties deploying AWS services or designing system architecture, the mentors always took the time to explain the root causes in detail and guide my thinking process toward finding a solution, rather than simply giving the answer.

The mentors also frequently shared real-world experience from working at enterprises, helping me better understand the process of developing and operating a system on the Cloud platform. These insights gave me a more practical view of a Cloud Engineer's job and a clearer direction for my future career path.

In addition, the Team Admin staff consistently provided quick support in sharing materials, announcing schedules, organizing events, and resolving issues related to the program. Thanks to this, I was able to focus on learning and completing projects without facing many obstacles during the Bootcamp.

---

### 3. Alignment Between the Internship and My Field of Study

In my opinion, the internship content at FCAJ is very well aligned with my major in Information Technology. While university coursework mainly covers foundational knowledge in programming, computer networks, and databases, the Bootcamp gave me direct exposure to technologies that are widely used in the industry today.

During the internship, I practiced with many Amazon Web Services (AWS) services such as EC2, S3, IAM, VPC, RDS, Lambda, API Gateway, CloudFront, and CloudWatch, among others. Directly deploying and configuring these services helped me better understand how to build and operate a system on the Cloud platform.

I also learned about the AWS Well-Architected Framework, multi-tier architecture design, CI/CD processes, and principles for optimizing systems on the Cloud. These are all highly applicable skills that helped me fill gaps not covered in depth by my university curriculum.

---

### 4. Opportunities for Learning and Skill Development

The time spent in the Bootcamp gave me many opportunities to learn. Besides technical knowledge about AWS Cloud, I also developed several important skills useful for my future career.

Through group projects, I learned how to plan work, divide tasks, coordinate with team members, and manage progress to meet deadlines. Whenever I encountered errors while deploying a system, I was guided on how to analyze the root cause, search for documentation, and resolve issues step by step instead of relying entirely on the mentors.

In addition, my skills in reading English technical documentation, presenting ideas, building AWS architecture diagrams, and reporting project results improved significantly. These skills will serve as an important foundation to help me adapt more easily to a professional working environment after graduation.

---

### 5. Culture and Team Spirit

What I loved most about FCAJ was the culture of knowledge-sharing among members. Everyone was always willing to support each other when facing difficulties and to discuss together to find the best solutions for the project.

While working in teams, each member was responsible for their own part of the work but always proactively supported others when needed. Mentors also always created opportunities for everyone to present ideas, discuss, and contribute opinions. This made me feel respected and gave me extra motivation to complete my assigned tasks well.

Although the Bootcamp duration was not very long, I could feel the sense of solidarity and professionalism within the FCAJ community. This is one of the aspects that left me with a very positive impression of the program.

---

## Personal Reflections After the Internship

After completing the FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY (FCAJ) Bootcamp, I feel that this was a very meaningful experience that brought great value to both my studies and my career direction. What satisfied me most was being part of a professional learning environment, getting hands-on practice with Amazon Web Services (AWS), and working together with team members to build a real project. Applying knowledge to actual system deployment helped me better understand how AWS services are used in practice, while also improving my thinking when designing and operating a Cloud architecture.

Beyond technical knowledge, I also learned many important skills such as teamwork, progress management, searching for technical documentation, presenting ideas, and solving problems that arose during project execution. These experiences are valuable assets that will help me feel more confident as I prepare to enter the workforce after graduation.

In my personal view, the Bootcamp program was quite well-structured, with a clear learning roadmap and dedicated support from the mentor team. However, I think the program could be even better with more sharing sessions from Cloud Engineers or Solution Architects currently working in the industry, as well as more large-scale, real-world projects to give trainees exposure to a wider range of practical situations.

If any friends or students are interested in Cloud Computing, DevOps, or AI, I would gladly recommend the FCAJ Bootcamp, as it is a high-quality learning environment with practical training content, dedicated mentors, and consistent support for trainees to grow both their technical knowledge and working skills.

In the future, if given the opportunity, I hope to continue participating in FCAJ's advanced training programs on AWS Solution Architecture, DevOps, or AI on AWS to further enhance my knowledge and skills. I also hope to continue accompanying the FCAJ community in academic activities or real-world projects to gain more experience for myself.

Finally, I would like to express my sincere thanks to the mentors, Teaching Assistants, admins, and the Organizing Committee of the FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY Bootcamp for their dedicated guidance, support, and for creating the conditions for me to learn in such a professional environment. My internship at FCAJ helped me grow in technical knowledge, working skills, and problem-solving mindset. This will serve as an important foundation as I continue to develop and pursue my goal of becoming a Cloud Engineer in the future.
