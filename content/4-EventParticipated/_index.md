During my internship, I had the opportunity to participate in two events. Each event was a memorable experience that provided valuable knowledge, meaningful insights, exciting gifts, and unforgettable moments.

## [Event 1](4.1-Event1/)

 **Event Name:** FCAJ Community Day

 **Time:** 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM, May 23, 2026

 **Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Sai Gon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

 **Role:** Attendee

### Brief Description

FCAJ Community Day is a community event organized by the **First Cloud A Journey (FCAJ)** group, bringing together nearly 400 participants. The event featured six technical sessions covering topics from Artificial Intelligence and Cloud Computing to real-world hackathon experiences:

- **Context Is Everything** – How providing the right context enables AI to perform effectively, along with the concept of a Second AI Brain.
- **Amazon QuickSight Q** – An AI-powered analytics tool that allows users to analyze data using natural language without writing queries.
- **CloudFront as Your Foundation** – Exploring Amazon CloudFront as a comprehensive platform for cost optimization, security (WAF, OAC), reliability, and performance.
- **36 hrs with LotusHacks** – A real-world story of a team building the UTMorpho product within a 36-hour hackathon.
- **Non-Determinism of LLM** – Understanding why Temperature = 0 does not guarantee consistent outputs and strategies for handling this challenge in production environments.
- **Multi-Agent System for Credit Scoring** – Designing a multi-agent architecture for startup credit evaluation, including guardrails and compliance considerations.

### Outcomes and Benefits

**Technical Knowledge:**

- Gained a deeper understanding of Amazon CloudFront, particularly OAC, caching strategies, and cost optimization techniques that can be directly applied to projects.
- Learned about the practical limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs), especially non-deterministic behavior, and approaches to building safer AI systems for production.
- Explored Multi-Agent Architecture as an effective pattern for solving complex AI-related problems.

**Soft Skills:**

- Improved prioritization and decision-making skills through lessons learned from a real hackathon experience.
- Better understood the importance of scope discipline and team communication under pressure.
- Learned how to evaluate trade-offs between product features and system stability.

**Community Value:**

- Gained practical insights from experienced professionals who have faced similar challenges, providing motivation to continue improving my projects.
- Recognized the importance of participating in professional communities to learn, share knowledge, and support personal and professional development.
